Thoughts on Progressive Enhancement

Progressive enhancement is an idea that focuses on the content of a wesbite and how to present and arrange that following content. The main end-goal for progressive enhancement is to have its users be able to access any content using any type of browser (either old or new).

I think progressive enhancement is extremely important in developing websites today because it allows any users to interact and access websites without them having to change their preferred browsing app. So, in a way, it's easier for the older generation to not adapt to newer settings. It can allow a user to be able to view the basic content of a website through the lens of both CSS and Javascript.

    • Segway Project Revision and Reflection

    For the revision process of my Segway Project, I asked my best friend, Natalie, to give me her feedback. I initially asked her out of all people to give me feedback because she is very honest with her thoughts which is why I trust her judgement. Her first suggestion was to change out the color font. She stated that it doesn't give out a modern or futuristic presentation because the color purple isn't something that a person thinks of when they think of advanced technology.

    The second suggestion was to look carefully at the formatting of the paragraphs. Some of the paragraphs weren't aligned to the center completelty because they were pushed more to the right hand side. The last feedback included moving the image of the Segway Centaur sketch to the very top. She stated that a first-time user will tend to feel confused if they read through the paragraphs without seeing the image at the beginning. With the image being in the top, a user will have more experience with the subject at-hand. One of the suggestions she made was erase the links of the content that a user is in currently. For example, removing the FAQ link when a user is in the FAQ content, but I didn't take that suggestion because I felt like it would distract the viewers too much from the page content.

    Fixing most of these suggestions were easy for me. For the color pallete, I only had to research what modern websites tend to look like. I used both google images and adobe color pallete as my research websites. Then I replaced my original colors with a variation of versions I liked best. For the content alignment issue, it was a little challenging for me to fix it. I previously tried using justify content to align my items to the center, but it didn't end up working. But soon after, I realized that the content was pushed to the side because they were listed as a list item. However, this problem was fixed quikcly after changing the items to a paragraph format. The last suggestion was changed just by copying and pasting the image above the paragraph content in the html file.
